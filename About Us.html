Background of the Training School

The Medical Laboratory Training School Jinja (MLTS) is a government-aided institution and registered by the National Council for Higher Education. It was established in 1967 as a training site for laboratory Attendants for Jinja Hospital.

The school is located at plots 3, 5 and 7 along Kaggwe road Jinja city on 5 acres of land. In 1976, the training of Laboratory Assistants commenced with only fifteen (15) students. In 1979, the liberation war disrupted the training and created a lull until 1985. In 1986, the training resumed with only nine (9) students. A three-year program was introduced to train graduates of Advanced Certificate Level. The Training division of the Ministry of Health granted the institution a diploma program whose pioneer group qualified in 1989.


The school strives to produce empathetic Laboratory Technicians and Assistants. Currently, the school is running two programs (Diploma in Medical Laboratory Technology and a Certificate in Medical Laboratory Techniques). Laboratory Technicians and assistants carry out laboratory tests for Diagnosis, Screening of diseases, and Patient management, including determining prognosis and therapy.

The student enrolment has steadily increased for both Diploma and Certificate levels, and the current enrolment stands at four hundred ninety-three (493) students, of which 354 are males and 139 females. The majority of 356 are Government-sponsored and 137 privately sponsored.

School's Mandate

The mandate of MLTSJ is to provide Medical Laboratory Technology Training particularly to provide; Equitable access to medical laboratory technology training and Accelerate the socio-economic transformation.

The School's mandate is derived from the 1995 constitution of Uganda, which provides for the right to education under Article 30; Business Technical Vocational Education and Training (BTVET) Act 2008; Universities and Other Tertiary Institutions Act 2003 (as amended).

Delivery of the MLTSJ mandate is guided within the existing policy framework, including;
a) The TVET policy 2019 and TVET policy implementation standards. These provide a framework for training, assessment and certification of all Health Training Institutions and;

b) The National Health Laboratory Policy (2009) policy objective 4 (5) provides laboratory services with an adequate number of skilled staff with the necessary competency and motivation to deliver quality laboratory services at all designated levels. Objective 4(8) encourages Laboratories to participate in relevant health research to improve patient management, laboratory performance and disease control. 

Management Structure 

The MLTS Governing Council is responsible for the provision of strategic direction, leadership and overall oversight. The Governing Council works through four committees, namely, Finance; Planning and Development; Student's Welfare; and Appointment/Disciplinary.

While the Guild President heads the student body.
The day-to-day operation of the school is the responsibility of the Principal and is assisted by Administrative and Teaching staff. The Principal carry's out the coordination function with all government agencies and engagement with other non-state actors.
