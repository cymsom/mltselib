Rules and Regulations at Medical Laboratory Training School Jinja (MLTSJ)

RULES
The following guidelines, principles, conditions shall govern behavior of all students:

Admission

1. (a) All students shall be residential
(b) Room and hostel allocation is strictly by the warden
(c) Married students can be allowed to stay in or out of the hostel. Should he/she stay in hostel, he/ she is not allowed to raise her/his family, friends etc in the hostel.

2. All students must attend all lectures, practical work and field work as stipulated without fail.
A student who absconds for 14 days will be discontinued
A student, granted permission of absence and later stays beyond the period permitted will be required to give and reasons for this and if found unsatisfactory he/she will be referred for a period of 6 months.(permission can be granted by Principal, D/principal, a tutor on duty, Registrar and the warden only). Cell phones, headphones and any other communication gadgets shall NOT be allowed in class, practicum site, failure to adhere will attract an appropriate penalty or confiscated for good. Students shall come for lectures 15 minutes before the lecture and after the stipulated time, no student shall be allowed in.

3. Negligence and Mishandling of patients
All trainees must attend to patients with all care and sympathy
No trainee should maliciously mistreat any patient physically or morally. Any student who falls short of this will be recalled to face the disciplinary committee of the school.

4. Possession and Administration of un prescribed drugs and stores equipment
No student is allowed to posses or administer any drug that is not dully prescribed by authorized person.
Trainees are also not expected to poses any chemicals, reagents, furniture and stores equipment that is not acquired through any authorized means

5. Drunkardness
Alcohol will not be tolerated in the school; anyone found drunk or in possession of unprescribed drug or alcohol substance, shall face the disciplinary committee of the school where an appropriate punishment will be preferred

6. Outing
The trainee is allowed to go out from 5.00 p.m. to 10.00 p.m. On week days and 7:00am to 10:00pm on weekends. Late passes may be granted only by the tutor in-charge.
Permission to sleep out shall be granted only after the tutor in-charge has received written consent of parent/guardian of the student.

7. Theft and Damage
Government and other personal properly must be handled with care and students found damaging or stealing such property will be charged to pay the cost of replacement of the article and be liable to further disciplinary action. No articles must be removed from or transferred from any room or place without permission from the Warden, the Principal or any Officer appointed by the Principal.
All articles issued to students must be accounted for and may be required to pay for any loses or malicious damage of property.

8. Fighting and Conflict
Fighting is forbidden. Any conflicts and violent acts between fellow students must be reported to the Guild President, Warden, and Tutor or Principal for settlement.
The parties involved will be liable for suspension or dismissal, pending the Governing Council decision.

9. Wearing Uniforms
All students must wear their lab coats while in class, on duty, in examinations and Public functions.
Students must dress decently. Tight fitting attire; mini skirts and blouses, T-shirts are not allowed in class and practical sites. Skirts should be at knee level. All students should wear name tags for identification in class and compound.
Boys MUST keep short and tidy hair and are NOT allowed to grow long beards; while the ladies are expected to keep their hair tidy befitting a medical student; male students are expected to put on neck ties. Any student who fails to adhere, will face the disciplinary committee of the school.

10. Attendance of dances and parties
No students are allowed to attend dances and parties outside the hostels or school without the permission of the tutor on duty.
All functions and recreation activities within the school premises including dances watching T.V. shall not go beyond 1.00 a.m. except under special circumstances, trainees will not normally serve in public functions unless with school permission.
In the hostels, lights must be switched off at 11.00 p.m. any student who fails to abide will face the disciplinary committee.

11. Sickness
Sick students must report to the school nurse/warden in-charge of the hostel except those staying outside the hostel. The students should report to the staff clinic for medical attention. Students attending hospital staff clinic must take with them an introduction form and this form must be returned to the office after getting treatment. Any student given sick leave or ward admission his/her parents will be called upon to take over immediately. A student will be referred in his or her course if continuously sick period for 21days. He/She will be required to make up for the period lost. Equally the school is given a mandate to discontinue a student found with serious medical problem.
Absenteeism from class for 14 cumulative days is liable to referral.

12. Immoral or unprofessional Behaviour
All trainees are expected to behave in a manner consistent width the Allied Professional Code of conduct. Any act that would ruin the good name of the profession will carry a penalty. Trainees are not expected to indulge in immoral behavior such as fornication, view of pornographic materials, use of vulgar language, nudity etc. a student who fails to abide will face disciplinary committee of the school, where an appropriate punishment will be preferred


13. Pregnancy
As soon as pregnancy is detected by Medical examination will lead to automatic discontinuation of the student from the training school. A female trainee who is discontinued because of pregnancy may be re-admitted after delivery provided she is well behaved, academically sound and has breast fed the baby for 6 months, the certificate of which should be produced. This privilege will be extended as a once and for all concession. Where a male student impregnates a female student in the school, his studies too should be discontinued and he re-applies for a re-admission 6 months after the birth of the baby. Both should make up for the period lost from the stage He/she was discontinued.
Any incidence of criminal abortions will be reported immediately to the nearest Police station.

14. Transfers
Once a trainee has been admitted in training school, there will be no transfer to another training school except on Medical grounds. Once a trainee is dismissed from any of the training schools, he/she will not be re-admitted to any of the other training schools

15. Examinations in Theory and Practical
The pass mark will be 50%. Progressive Assessment tests will be held from time to time including the end of term examinations. Any student whose performance is not satisfactory will be warned and if they continue to show lack of progress they should be referred or discontinued. However, failure in three consecutive semester examinations in any academic year will lead to automatic discontinuation from the course. A student who fails three or more subjects at the end of year one semester one exams will be dismissed. Any form of examination malpractice will lead to immediate discontinuation of the student.
Other details on various rules and regulations shall be developed by the Academic Committee and take the task to advice the Examination Board accordingly.

16. Payment of dues
(a) On the first day of arrival at the school of every semester, a student shall be required to deposit in the bank account at least ¾ or 75% of the dues as security against debts to the school
Payment of caution money shall be reviewed at the beginning of the academic year whenever necessary
(b) Tuition fees , registration fees shall be paid in full on the first day of the academic year
Examination fees shall be paid before expiry of deadline set by the Uganda Allied Health Examinations Board
(c) Every student shall pay subscriptions to the students guild bank account on the first day of arrival in the school
(d) A student who is a debtor to the school may not be allowed to sit for examinations or progress to the next class or receive a diploma award
A student is a debtor to the school if he/ she has not paid whole or part of the fees for registration, accommodation and tuition or when he owes the library, laboratory and any other department.


REGULATIONS
The following controls shall be followed by all students:
1. Personal property and place of residence
Each trainee is responsible for his/ her valuables and personal property. They must keep and maintain their places of residence clean and tidy. All trainees must participate in keeping their classes / laboratories clean after use and disinfect work places after practical.
2. Bathrooms and toilets
To avoid blockage of the sewers (waste drain pipes) no rubbish, food particles, hair or used sanitary towel should be thrown into sinks, toilet bowels. These should be placed in waste bins provided around the dormitories. Each trainee is expected to use and leave the toilets, showers and sinks clean, while avoiding wastage of water. Report any water leakages to the warden or dormitory representative immediately. Sanitary towels shall be disposed off in sanitary bins provided in female dormitories.
3. Laundry
Trainees are to do their personal washing in small laundries provided or other spaces provided for the purpose. Any equipment for repair or exchange should be handed over to the Principal / responsible person according to the procedure of segregation of duties in the school.
4. Meal times
Meals shall be served at the following times:
Breakfast 6:30 am - 7:30 am
Mid morning break 11:00 am - 11:15 am
Lunch 1:00 pm - 2:00 pm
Supper 6:30 pm - 8:00 pm
All meals should be taken in the dinning hall. Food should not be taken to the dormitories to reduce spread of vermin. Cooking is not permitted in the dormitories. All cooking utensils and un authorized electrical appliances shall be confiscated from the student by any member of the school authority and brought to the attention of the disciplinary committee. Students who request for special diets should provide medical evidence prescribed by a recognized medical officer. No trainee is allowed to provide food to the visitors at any one time.
5. WATER AND ELECTRICITY
(a) Water taps, gas taps should be turned off tightly after use to avoid leakage
(b) All lights must be switched off when not in use in the dormitories, common rooms, class rooms, toilets. The last trainee to leave any room with lights should switch of the lights.
Electric flat irons may be used in the laundry and designated places but not in the bed rooms. Exercise care to avoid accidents when using the flat irons.
6. Out of bounds
The hospital is out of bounds to all trainees except when on study duty or sick. Sick students should be visited during official visiting hours only.
The kitchen, workshop, store and tutors residences are out of bounds except during emergency or with permission of the officers concerned.
7. Visitors
Trainees receive visitors ONLY in the visitors shade during visiting times at 12:45 pm to 2:00 pm and 4:00 pm
8. Evening study
All trainees must attend evening preps between 8:00 pm – 10:00 pm from Monday to Friday in the classrooms and all students should observe silence
9. Punctuality
All trainees must be punctual at all times and places. They must report on duty and when reporting off duty, hand over to the officer in charge and before leaving the department or school for any purpose. A student who comes after 15 minutes after the designated time for a lecture / practicum will not be allowed in class or clinical area.
10. Noise
Unnecessary noise, loud music must be avoided at any place and at all times by all students. Play music that is as loud as it goes to your ears only!
11. Disciplinary action
All trainees are strongly required to abide by the above rules and regulations during the entire period of training
Trainees must abide by any rules and regulations that are made from time to time
Any students or student leader who misappropriates public funds entrusted to him or her misuses, vandalizes any of the school properties shall be guilty of an offence and shall be liable for disciplinary action
Breach of any of the rules and regulations will lead to appropriate disciplinary action against the trainee.
The following are disciplinary measures; depending on the magnitude of the offence committed
(a) verbal warning
(b) Written warning
(c) Referral
(d) Suspension
(e) Dismissal

12. Correspondences
All forms of correspondences by trainees to Higher and other Authorities MUST pass through the Principal
